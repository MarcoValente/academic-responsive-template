Hello! I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at TRIUMF, Canada's particle accelerator centre. I am currently based at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) and my research focuses on the searches for new physics carried out by the ATLAS experiment, one of the four main physics experiments of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). This webpage illustrates my research, teaching and outreach activities. Further details about my research interests are provided below in the research tab. For any additional question or information about my work, do not hesitate to contact me following the informations provided in the contact section below!

Research interests:

Supersymmetry R-parity violating (RPV) SUSY Particle Flow Jets Missing Transverse Momentum (MET) Triggers HL-LHC Machine Learning Future Colliders

Despite the extremely precise description of subatomic phenomena provided by the Standard Model, several open questions of physics are still waiting for an answer such as the origin of dark matter, the Higgs hierarchy mass problem and the unification of elementary forces at high energy. Answering these questions defines my research program at the ATLAS experiment:

  • Searches for strong supersymmetric particles and Dark Matter.
  • Reconstruction of jets and Missing Transverse Momentum (MET).
  • Hadronic-event triggering
