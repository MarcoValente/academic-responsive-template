Hello! I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at TRIUMF, Canada's particle accelerator centre. I am currently based at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) and my research focuses on the searches for new physics carried out by the ATLAS experiment, one of the four main physics experiments of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). This webpage illustrates my research, teaching and outreach activities. Further details about my research interests are provided below in the research tab. For any additional question or information about my work, do not hesitate to contact me following the informations provided in the contact section!

Research interests:

Supersymmetry Particle Flow Jets Missing Transverse Momentum (MET) Triggers HL-LHC Future Colliders

Despite the extremely precise description of subatomic phenomena provided by the Standard Model, several questions of physics are still open today such as the origin of Dark Matter, the matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe and the unification of elementary forces at high energy. Look for answers to these questions defines my research activities at the ATLAS experiment. These activities include:

  • Searches for Higgs-boson pair production and strong supersymmetric particles in hadronic final states.
  • Reconstruction and triggering of jets and Missing Transverse Momentum (MET).
  • Silicon strip detectors for the ATLAS HL-LHC Inner Tracker (ITk) upgrade.
